    What is AI coding capable of?

    So what is AI coding capable of? According to a blog post from Max Struever (engineer and consultant for over ten years), Matthew Krop (CIO of BCG X) and Julie Bedard (Researcher at BCG X) AI is capable of many things. Both Microsoft and Google's new code is around 30% AI. AI is very good at streamlining repetitive and low priority tasks. This means that tedious tasks can be handled by AI in the background, saving developers time on more important tasks. AI won't give huge improvements overnight though, as you still need to train and develop the AI's memory and tasks. Hitachi (Multi industry company involved with software development) reports that their workers work 83% faster with AI helping them. These facts give a lot of insight into the research, but the source itself does have its strengths and weaknesses. It is quite good with the facts, giving numbers and data, but its not all good. All 3 of the writers from this article work within the company that published the article. This means that this entire article could be working for them in some way. The stats in this article aren't just random numbers, they are significant improvements. Huge companies like Microsoft use a lot of AI in their new code (⅓). This means that ⅓ of the work force put onto the developers has been lifted, making them have to do a lot less work, and allows them to focus on much more important things that AI can't. Hitachi devs working 83% faster mean they are working almost twice the speed with AI. This means that production is almost doubled, massively improving profit margins and allowing developers to work through their work much faster than they ever could. These facts show us, or at least give us a glimpse into what ai is capable of. It shows us that production speed can be massively increased, and work stress also massively decreases.
